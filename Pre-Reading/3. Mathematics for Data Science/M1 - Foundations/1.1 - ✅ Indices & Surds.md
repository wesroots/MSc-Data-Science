> [!note] Topic Overview
> ## Definition
> - Indices are powers/exponents, surds are irrational roots left in exact form (not rounded)
> - Core models are built on exponential functions
>
> #maths 

# Indices
## Laws of indices
- Multiplication -> $a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$
- Division -> $\tfrac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$
- Power of a power -> $(a^m)^n = a^{m \times n}$ 
- Zero index -> $a^0 = 1$
- Negative indices -> $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$
## Fractional indices
- $a^{1/n} = \sqrt[n]{a}$
- $a^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{a^m}$

# Surds
## Basic surds
- $\sqrt ab = \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b}$
- $\sqrt{\tfrac{a}{b}} = \tfrac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}$
- $\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{a}} = \tfrac{\sqrt{a}}{a}$
## Simplifying
- Extracting factors: e.g. $\sqrt{72} = \sqrt{36 \times 2} = 6 \sqrt{2}$
- Addition and subtraction of surds
	- Like terms only $2 \sqrt{3} + 5 \sqrt{3} = 7 \sqrt{3}$
	- Unlike surds cannot be simplified together: $\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3}$
## Multiplication and division
- $\sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{ab}$
- $\frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}} = \sqrt{\tfrac{a}{b}}$
